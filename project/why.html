Importance

Diversity of Web Users

Ensuring a web for everyone, means people with different abilities can use the web to the capability it's intended for. Every person is different and unique, we all have diverse abilities, preferences, skills, etc and this all affects the way we use technology.

Because everyone has different abilities, we have to consider different things. One is age-related impairments, where older people struggle to use technology. We also consider individuals with multiple disabilities, which can affect how they interact with digital devices. Individuals with health conditions that can affect their concentration, stamina, etc also have to be considered because their symptoms can impact how they use technology and for how long. Others can experience recurring functional limitations that affect their use of technology. Some people are temporarily impaired and struggle to use technology. There also exists situational limitations where there are impairments in using technology in different situations, like not being able to hear audio, etc.

Why do we Need it?

The diversity of web users is greatly spread out and not just based on disabilities, so it is important to use universal design guidelines to build websites that everyone can use and enjoy despite their limitations.

The World Health Organization estimates 16% of the world's population experience disabilities. This is about 1.3 billion people. The number only continues to rise with the growing population and with an aging population. In the United States, 1 in 4 people have a disability.

It is increasingly important to make sure technology is accessible for everyone and it can be utilized by everyone. Much of the world is increasingly using technology, and leaving behind a population by not making the web accessible is leaving them at a disadvantage and is not equitable. By applying universal design on webpages, we can ensure that all users are able to fully maximize the capabilities of the Internet.
